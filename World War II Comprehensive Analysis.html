Chronological Analysis: WWII Key Events Timeline

September 1, 1939

Germany invades Poland, triggering declarations of war from Britain and France. World War II begins in Europe.

June 1940

Fall of France. Dunkirk evacuation saves 338,000 Allied troops. Britain stands alone against Nazi Germany.

December 7, 1941

Japan attacks Pearl Harbor, bringing the United States into the war. Global conflict now involves all major powers.

February 2, 1943

German surrender at Stalingrad. First major German defeat marks turning point on Eastern Front.

June 6, 1944

D-Day landings in Normandy. Allied forces establish Western Front in Europe.

May 8, 1945

V-E Day (Victory in Europe). Germany surrenders unconditionally, ending war in Europe.

August 15, 1945

V-J Day (Victory over Japan). Japan surrenders after atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
